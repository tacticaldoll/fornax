# Durability Reference

Use this reference to judge whether knowledge is ready to persist.

## Durability Signals

Knowledge is durable when it has:

- Clear future use.
- Stable wording or decision outcome.
- Enough source support to survive outside the original conversation.
- A natural owner or destination.
- A maintenance path when it changes.

## Fragility Signals

Delay or decline persistence when the content is:

- A first impression.
- A personal preference without operational consequence.
- A speculative idea that has not been tested.
- A report-style reflection better suited for internalization.
- A host-specific workaround being proposed as a portable rule.
- A duplicate of existing knowledge.

## Reasoning Preservation

Preserve reasoning when future users need to understand trade-offs, exceptions, or migration
constraints. Otherwise, write the durable conclusion directly and keep the surrounding explanation
short.
